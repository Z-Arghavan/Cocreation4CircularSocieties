LCA stands for Life Cycle Assessment

LCA stands for Life Cycle Assessment. It is a systematic method used to evaluate the environmental impact of a product, process, or service throughout its entire lifecycle. The impacts are reported in terms of carbon emissions (amongst other impact categories). Therefore, we can compare products through LCA.
The assessment typically covers 5 life cycle stages:

  1. Raw Material Extraction: Evaluates the impact of obtaining the raw materials used in the product.
  2. Manufacturing: Assesses the energy and resources required to make the product and any pollution resulting from the production processes.
  3. Transportation: Considers the environmental impact of transporting raw materials and the finished product.
  4. Use Phase: Looks at the impact of using the product, including energy consumption and emissions during its use.
  5. End-of-Life: Assesses disposal, recycling, or reuse of the product, including the impacts of waste treatment.
